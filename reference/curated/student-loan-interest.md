# Student Loan Interest Deduction

## Source
- Publication 970 (2025), "Tax Benefits for Education," Chapter 4
- Instructions for Schedule 1 (Form 1040) (2025), Line 21
- Form 1040 Instructions (2025), Page 6

## Applicable To
- **Forms:** Schedule 1, Line 21; Form 1040, Line 10
- **Workflow Steps:** Step 4 (Adjustments to Income)

---

## Rules

### Basic Rule
- You can deduct **up to $2,500** of interest paid on qualified student loans as an above-the-line adjustment to income. [Pub 970 (2025), Chapter 4]
- This is an adjustment (not an itemized deduction) — available whether you take the standard deduction or itemize.
- Report on Schedule 1, Line 21, which flows to Form 1040, Line 10.

### Qualified Student Loan
- A loan taken out solely to pay qualified education expenses (tuition, fees, room and board, etc.) for you, your spouse, or a dependent.
- The loan cannot be from a related person or made under a qualified employer plan.

### Eligibility Requirements
- Filing status cannot be MFS (Married Filing Separately).
- You cannot be claimed as a dependent on another person's return.
- The student must have been enrolled at least half-time in a degree program.

### MAGI Phase-Out (MFJ)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Phase-out begins | $170,000 MAGI |
| Phase-out ends (fully eliminated) | $200,000 MAGI |
| Maximum deduction | $2,500 |

[Pub 970 (2025), Chapter 4]

- For Single/HoH: Phase-out is $80,000–$95,000 MAGI.
- **If MAGI ≥ $200,000 (MFJ), the deduction is completely eliminated — $0.**

### MAGI Definition (for this deduction)
- MAGI = AGI + foreign earned income exclusion + foreign housing exclusion/deduction + excluded income from sources in Puerto Rico or American Samoa.
- For most domestic filers, MAGI = AGI.

### Form 1098-E
- Lenders send Form 1098-E showing the student loan interest paid during the year (Box 1).
- Only report interest that qualifies.
