# Additional Medicare Tax — Form 8959

## Source
- Form 1040 Instructions (2025), Schedule 2 Instructions, Line 11 — pp. 111–113
- Bipartisan Policy Center, "2025 Federal Income Tax Brackets," Table 7
- IRC §3101(b)(2) (Additional Medicare Tax)

## Applicable To
- **Forms:** Form 8959 (all lines); Schedule 2, Line 11; Form 1040, Lines 23 (via Schedule 2, Line 21) and 25d (withholding credit)
- **Workflow Steps:** Step 1 (W-2 Income — flag Medicare wages), Step 8 (Credits & Other Taxes), Step 9 (Payments — withholding credit)

---

## Rules

### Who Must File Form 8959
- File Form 8959 if Medicare wages (W-2 Box 5) or self-employment income exceeds the threshold for your filing status. [Form 8959 Instructions]
- Also file if any employer withheld Additional Medicare Tax (W-2 Box 14 code "Additional Medicare Tax").

### Tax Rate and Thresholds

| Filing Status | Threshold | Rate |
|--------------|-----------|------|
| Married Filing Jointly (MFJ) | $250,000 | 0.9% |
| Married Filing Separately (MFS) | $125,000 | 0.9% |
| Single / Head of Household | $200,000 | 0.9% |

[IRC §3101(b)(2); Bipartisan Policy Center, Table 7]

### Form 8959 Line-by-Line

**Part I — Additional Medicare Tax on Medicare Wages (Lines 1–7)**
- **Line 1:** Total Medicare wages and tips from all W-2s (Box 5). Combine both spouses if MFJ.
- **Line 2:** Unreported tips from Form 4137 (if any).
- **Line 3:** Wages from Form 8919 (if any).
- **Line 4:** Add Lines 1 + 2 + 3.
- **Line 5:** Enter threshold ($250,000 for MFJ).
- **Line 6:** Subtract Line 5 from Line 4 (if zero or less, enter 0).
- **Line 7:** Additional Medicare Tax on wages = **Line 6 × 0.9%** (0.009).

**Part II — Additional Medicare Tax on Self-Employment Income (Lines 8–13)**
- **Line 8:** Self-employment income from Schedule SE, Part I, Line 6 (if loss, enter 0).
- **Line 9:** Enter threshold ($250,000 for MFJ).
- **Line 10:** Enter the amount from Line 4 (total Medicare wages from Part I).
- **Line 11:** Subtract Line 10 from Line 9 (if zero or less, enter 0). This is the remaining threshold after wages.
- **Line 12:** Subtract Line 11 from Line 8 (if zero or less, enter 0). This is SE income subject to additional tax.
- **Line 13:** Additional Medicare Tax on SE income = **Line 12 × 0.9%** (0.009).
- With a Schedule C net loss, Line 8 is $0, so this entire section is $0.

**Part III — Additional Medicare Tax on Railroad Retirement (RRTA) Compensation (Lines 14–17)** — N/A for most filers.

**Part IV — Total Additional Medicare Tax**
- **Line 18:** Sum of Lines 7 + 13 + 17 → enters Schedule 2, Line 11 → adds to total tax.

**Withholding Credit (Part V, Lines 19–24)**
- **Line 19:** Total Medicare tax withheld from all W-2s (Box 6).
- **Line 20:** Enter the amount from Line 1 (Medicare wages, NOT the threshold).
- **Line 21:** Line 20 × 1.45% = regular Medicare tax on your actual wages.
- **Line 22:** Line 19 − Line 21 (if zero or less, enter 0). This is your Additional Medicare Tax withholding credit.
- **Line 24:** Total Additional Medicare Tax withholding (Lines 22 + 23) → included on **Form 1040, Line 25c**.

### How Employer Withholding Works
- Employers are required to withhold 0.9% Additional Medicare Tax on wages exceeding $200,000 for **each individual employee** (not combined for MFJ). [IRC §3102(f)]
- The employer withholding threshold is $200,000 per worker, regardless of filing status.
- For MFJ, the actual liability threshold is $250,000 on **combined** wages.
- This mismatch means: if each spouse earns under $200K but combined exceeds $250K, the employer may not have withheld enough → balance due on Form 8959.
