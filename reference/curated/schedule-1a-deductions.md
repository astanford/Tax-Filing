# Schedule 1-A — New OBBBA Deductions (2025)

## Source
- Instructions for Schedule 1-A (Form 1040) (2025) — Form 1040 Instructions, pp. 101–115
- One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA), H.R. 1, signed July 4, 2025
- H&R Block, "One Big Beautiful Bill Act Tax Impacts," accessed Mar 2, 2026

## Applicable To
- **Forms:** Schedule 1-A (Lines 4–38); Form 1040, Line 13b
- **Workflow Steps:** Step 6 (Deductions — Standard vs Itemized)

---

## Rules

### General
- Schedule 1-A provides **four new deductions** enacted by OBBBA, effective **2025–2028**. [Form 1040 Instructions (2025), Page 101]
- These deductions are available whether you claim the standard deduction OR itemize on Schedule A. They are **in addition to** your chosen deduction method.
- The total from Schedule 1-A, Line 38 enters **Form 1040, Line 13b**.
- MAGI is computed in Part I of Schedule 1-A (generally = Form 1040, Line 11b for domestic filers).

### Deduction 1: No Tax on Tips (Part II)
- Deduct up to **$25,000** of qualified tips received in 2025. [Form 1040 Instructions (2025), Page 101]
- **Qualified tips:** Cash tips (including credit card tips) paid voluntarily by customers in occupations that customarily received tips as of Dec 31, 2024.
- **NOT qualified:** Service charges, automatic gratuities, mandatory amounts, tips from SSTBs (with transition relief for 2025).
- **MAGI phase-out:** Begins at $300,000 (MFJ) / $150,000 (Single).
- **SSN required.** If married, must file jointly.
- **Tipped occupations list:** Includes wait staff, bartenders, hairdressers, taxi/rideshare drivers, delivery workers, hotel staff, etc. Full list at IRS.gov/TippedOccupations.

### Deduction 2: No Tax on Overtime (Part III)
- Deduct up to **$12,500** ($25,000 MFJ if both spouses have qualifying overtime) of qualified overtime compensation. [Form 1040 Instructions (2025), Page 106]
- Covers overtime wages required to be paid at a premium rate under the **Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)**.
- Employer must designate overtime on W-2 (for 2025, employers may approximate).
- **MAGI phase-out:** Begins at $300,000 (MFJ) / $150,000 (Single).
- **SSN required.** If married, must file jointly.
- **Not available for:** Exempt employees (salaried/managerial not covered by FLSA overtime), self-employed, MFS filers.

### Deduction 3: No Tax on Car Loan Interest (Part IV)
- Deduct up to **$10,000** of qualified passenger vehicle loan interest on a vehicle purchased in 2025 for personal use. [Form 1040 Instructions (2025), Page 108]
- Vehicle must have **final assembly in the United States**.
- Must include the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) on the return.
- **MAGI phase-out:** Begins at $200,000 (MFJ) / $100,000 (Single). Reduced by 20% of excess.
- Cannot be for a vehicle used in a trade or business (those go on Schedule C).

### Deduction 4: Enhanced Deduction for Seniors (Part V)
- Additional deduction of up to **$6,000** ($12,000 if both spouses age 65+) for taxpayers born before January 2, 1961. [Form 1040 Instructions (2025), Page 110]
- This is **on top of** the standard deduction (or itemized) and the existing additional standard deduction for age 65+.
- **MAGI phase-out:** Begins at $150,000 (MFJ) / $75,000 (Single).
- **SSN required.** If married, must file jointly. Not available for MFS.
- **Effective:** 2025–2028.

### MAGI Phase-Out Summary

| Deduction | Max Amount (MFJ) | Phase-out Begins (MFJ) | Phase-out Begins (Single) |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| Tips | $25,000 | $300,000 | $150,000 |
| Overtime | $25,000 (combined) | $300,000 | $150,000 |
| Car Loan Interest | $10,000 | $200,000 | $100,000 |
| Senior | $12,000 (both 65+) | $150,000 | $75,000 |
