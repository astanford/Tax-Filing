# Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT) — Form 8960

## Source
- Instructions for Form 8960 (2025) — [IRS.gov/instructions/i8960](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i8960)
- IRS Topic No. 559, Net Investment Income Tax — [IRS.gov/taxtopics/tc559](https://www.irs.gov/taxtopics/tc559)
- IRS Q&A on the Net Investment Income Tax — [IRS.gov/newsroom](https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/questions-and-answers-on-the-net-investment-income-tax)
- IRC §1411 (Imposition of Tax)

## Applicable To
- **Forms:** Form 8960 (all lines); Schedule 2, Line 12; Form 1040, Line 23 (via Schedule 2, Line 21)
- **Workflow Steps:** Step 2 (Investment Income), Step 8 (Credits & Other Taxes)

---

## Rules

### Who Must File Form 8960
- File Form 8960 if your modified adjusted gross income (MAGI) exceeds the threshold for your filing status AND you have net investment income. [Form 8960 Instructions (2025)]
- The tax applies to individuals, estates, and trusts. [IRC §1411]

### Tax Rate and Thresholds

| Filing Status | Threshold | Rate |
|--------------|-----------|------|
| Married Filing Jointly (MFJ) | $250,000 | 3.8% |
| Married Filing Separately (MFS) | $125,000 | 3.8% |
| Single / Head of Household | $200,000 | 3.8% |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | $250,000 | 3.8% |

These thresholds are **not inflation-adjusted** — they have been $250K/$125K/$200K since 2013. [IRC §1411(b)]

### What Is Net Investment Income (NII)

NII includes:
- **Interest** (taxable interest from Form 1040, Line 2b) [Form 8960 Instructions, Line 1]
- **Dividends** (ordinary dividends from Form 1040, Line 3b) [Form 8960 Instructions, Line 2]
- **Capital gains** (net gain from Form 1040, Line 7, including short-term and long-term) [Form 8960 Instructions, Line 5a]
- **Rental and royalty income** (passive activity income from Schedule E) [Form 8960 Instructions, Line 4a]
- **Passive business income** (from partnerships/S corps where taxpayer does not materially participate) [IRS Q&A]
- **Non-qualified annuity income** [Form 8960 Instructions, Line 3]

### What Is NOT Net Investment Income

NII does **not** include:
- **Wages and salaries** (W-2 income) [IRS Topic 559]
- **Self-employment income from active businesses** (Schedule C where taxpayer materially participates) [IRS Q&A; Form 8960 Instructions, Line 4b]
- **Tax-exempt interest** (municipal bond interest) [IRS Topic 559]
- **Social Security benefits** [IRS Topic 559]
- **Unemployment compensation** [IRS Topic 559]
- **Distributions from qualified retirement plans** (401(a), 403(a), 403(b), 408/408A IRAs, 457(b)) [IRS Q&A]
- **Gain excluded under section 121** (primary residence sale, up to $500K MFJ) [IRS Q&A]
- **Alaska Permanent Fund Dividends** [Form 8960 Instructions, Line 2]

### MAGI Definition for NIIT
- MAGI = Adjusted Gross Income (Form 1040, Line 11) **plus** any foreign earned income exclusion under section 911. [Form 8960 Instructions, Line 13]
- For domestic filers with no foreign earned income exclusion, MAGI = AGI. [Form 8960 Instructions, Line 13]

### Form 8960 Line-by-Line

**Part I — Net Investment Income**
- **Line 1:** Taxable interest (from Form 1040, Line 2b).
- **Line 2:** Ordinary dividends (from Form 1040, Line 3b). Excludes ESOP dividends and Alaska Permanent Fund.
- **Line 3:** Annuities (non-qualified only; excludes retirement plan distributions).
- **Line 4a:** Income from trades/businesses, rental real estate, royalties, partnerships, S corps, trusts (Schedule C, E, F).
- **Line 4b:** Adjustment for net income/loss from active (non-passive) trades or businesses. Enter as negative to exclude active business income; enter as positive to add back active business losses. [Form 8960 Instructions, Line 4b]
- **Line 5a:** Net gain/loss from capital asset dispositions (Form 1040, Line 7) plus ordinary gains (Schedule 1, Line 4).
- **Lines 5b–5d:** Adjustments for excluded gains (non-section 1411 business property, NUA, CFC/QEF gains).
- **Lines 6–7:** CFC/PFIC adjustments and other modifications (section 1411 NOL carryforwards, etc.).
- **Line 8:** Total investment income (sum of Lines 1–7).

**Part II — Investment Expenses Allocable to Investment Income**
- **Line 9a:** Investment interest expense (section 163(d)).
- **Line 9b:** State, local, and foreign income tax allocable to investment income.
- **Line 9c:** Miscellaneous investment expenses (advisory fees, custodial fees, section 212 expenses).
- **Line 10:** Additional modifications (trader expenses, early withdrawal penalties).
- **Line 11:** Total deductions (sum of Lines 9–10).

**Line 12:** Net investment income = Line 8 minus Line 11.

**Part III — Tax Computation (Individuals)**
- **Line 13:** Modified Adjusted Gross Income (AGI + section 911 adjustments).
- **Line 14:** Threshold ($250,000 for MFJ).
- **Line 15:** Excess MAGI = Line 13 minus Line 14 (if zero or less, enter 0).
- **Line 16:** Smaller of Line 12 (NII) or Line 15 (MAGI excess).
- **Line 17:** NIIT = Line 16 × 3.8% (0.038). → **Schedule 2, Line 12.**

### Interaction with Additional Medicare Tax (Form 8959)
- NIIT and Additional Medicare Tax are **separate taxes** that do not overlap. [IRS Q&A]
- Additional Medicare Tax (0.9%) applies to **wages and self-employment income** (Form 8959).
- NIIT (3.8%) applies to **investment income** (Form 8960).
- Both can apply in the same year if income exceeds both thresholds. [IRS Q&A]
- Both flow to Schedule 2 (Lines 12 and 11 respectively) → Form 1040, Line 23.

### Schedule C Active Business Exclusion
- If you operate a trade or business in which you materially participate (non-passive under section 469), the net income is excluded from NII via Line 4b. [Form 8960 Instructions, Line 4b]
- Conversely, losses from active businesses are also excluded — an active business loss does NOT reduce NII. The Line 4b adjustment adds the loss back. [Form 8960 Instructions, Line 4b]
- Passive business income (where you do NOT materially participate) IS included in NII. [IRS Q&A]
