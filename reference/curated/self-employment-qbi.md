## Self-Employment Tax Rules

### When SE Tax Applies
- You must pay SE tax if net earnings from self-employment exceed **$400**. [IRC §1402]
- SE tax covers Social Security (12.4% up to $176,100 wage base) and Medicare (2.9%, no cap).
- If you also have W-2 wages, only the portion of self-employment income that, combined with wages, falls under the SS wage base is subject to the 12.4% SS portion.

### SE Tax Calculation
1. Start with Schedule C net profit.
2. Multiply by **92.35%** (0.9235) to get net SE earnings. This adjustment accounts for the employer-equivalent portion. [IRC §1402(a)]
3. Apply 15.3% SE tax rate (12.4% SS + 2.9% Medicare) to net SE earnings.
4. If net SE earnings + W-2 wages exceed $176,100, only the excess above wages (up to $176,100 combined) is subject to the 12.4% SS portion.
5. The **employer-equivalent portion** (50% of SE tax) is deductible on Schedule 1, Line 15 — this reduces AGI.

### Schedule SE Not Required If:
- Schedule C shows a **net loss** (net profit ≤ $400). [IRC §1402]
- No SE tax is due and Schedule SE is not filed.

## QBI Deduction Rules

### Basic Rule
- Eligible taxpayers may deduct up to **20%** of their qualified business income (QBI) from a qualified trade or business operated as a sole proprietorship, partnership, or S corporation. [IRC §199A]
- The deduction is taken on Form 1040, Line 13a — it reduces taxable income but NOT AGI.
- Available to both itemizers and standard deduction filers.

### Which Form to Use

| Situation | Form |
|-----------|------|
| Taxable income ≤ $394,600 (MFJ) and no SSTB or aggregation issues | **Form 8995** (Simplified) |
| Taxable income > $394,600 (MFJ) or has SSTB or aggregation | **Form 8995-A** (Regular) |

[Form 8995 Instructions]

### Simplified Computation (Form 8995)
1. **Line 1:** Enter QBI from each qualified business (Schedule C net profit or loss for sole proprietors).
2. **Line 2:** QBI deduction = 20% of QBI (or $0 if QBI is negative/zero).
3. **Line 10:** Deduction is the **lesser of:** (a) total QBI deduction from all businesses, or (b) 20% of taxable income before QBI deduction.
4. **Line 15:** Enter the deduction on Form 1040, Line 13a.

### Taxable Income Threshold (MFJ)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Full deduction available below | $394,600 (MFJ) |
| Phase-out range | $394,600 – $494,600 (MFJ) |
| SSTB exclusion applies above | $494,600 (MFJ) |

[IRC §199A(e)(2)]

- Below the threshold: Full 20% deduction, no SSTB limitations.
- Above the threshold: W-2 wage and capital limitations apply; SSTBs phase out.

### Specified Service Trades or Businesses (SSTBs)
- SSTBs include health, law, accounting, actuarial science, performing arts, consulting, athletics, financial services, brokerage, and any business where the principal asset is the reputation or skill of the owners/employees. [IRC §199A(d)(2)]
- If taxable income is below $394,600 (MFJ), **SSTB classification does not matter** — full deduction applies.
- **Retail/resale and manufacturing activities are NOT SSTBs** — only the listed service categories qualify.

### QBI Loss Carryforward
- If a qualified business has a **net loss**, the loss is treated as **negative QBI** and carries forward to reduce QBI in future years. [IRC §199A(c)(2)]
- The loss carryforward reduces the QBI of that business (or aggregate QBI if you have multiple businesses) in subsequent years.
- **File Form 8995 even with $0 deduction** to establish the loss carryforward on the record. [Form 8995 Instructions, Line 16]
- **Line 16 of Form 8995:** Report the total QBI carryforward amount.

### QBI Loss Carryforward Mechanics
- The carryforward is **not** a net operating loss (NOL). It is a separate QBI concept.
- In future years, the carryforward reduces the 20% deduction base, not AGI.
- Example: If 2025 QBI = -$594 and 2026 QBI = +$2,000, the 2026 QBI for deduction purposes = $2,000 - $594 = $1,406. Deduction = 20% × $1,406 = $281.
