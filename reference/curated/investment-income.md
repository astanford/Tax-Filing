# Investment Income — Interest, Dividends, and Capital Gains

## Source
- Instructions for Schedule B (Form 1040) (2025) — IRS.gov/ScheduleB
- Form 1040 Instructions (2025), Lines 2a/2b, 3a/3b, 7 — pp. 25–33
- Publication 550 (2024), "Investment Income and Expenses"
- IRC §1211(b) (capital loss limitation)

## Applicable To
- **Forms:** Form 1040 Lines 2a, 2b, 3a, 3b, 7; Schedule B; Schedule D; Form 8949
- **Workflow Steps:** Step 2 (Investment Income)

---

## Rules

### Interest Income (Lines 2a, 2b)

#### Taxable Interest (Line 2b)
- Report all taxable interest on Line 2b. Sources include banks, CDs, brokerage accounts, U.S. Treasury securities. [Form 1040 Instructions (2025), Page 25]
- Each payer sends Form 1099-INT. Enter the total on Line 2b.

#### Tax-Exempt Interest (Line 2a)
- Municipal bond interest and exempt-interest dividends from mutual funds. [Form 1040 Instructions (2025), Page 25]
- Shown in Box 8 of Form 1099-INT or Box 12 of Form 1099-DIV.
- Reported on Line 2a for informational purposes — not taxed federally.

#### Schedule B Required
- You must file **Schedule B** if total taxable interest exceeds **$1,500** OR total ordinary dividends exceed **$1,500**. [Schedule B Instructions (2025)]
- Also required if you had a foreign account or received a distribution from a foreign trust.

### Dividends (Lines 3a, 3b)

#### Ordinary Dividends (Line 3b)
- Total ordinary dividends from Form 1099-DIV, Box 1a. [Form 1040 Instructions (2025), Page 26]
- Schedule B required if total exceeds $1,500.

#### Qualified Dividends (Line 3a)
- Qualified dividends are a subset of ordinary dividends, taxed at preferential QDCG rates (0%/15%/20%). [Form 1040 Instructions (2025), Page 25]
- Shown in Form 1099-DIV, Box 1b.
- **Holding period requirement:** Must hold the stock for more than 60 days during the 121-day period beginning 60 days before the ex-dividend date. [Pub 550 (2024)]

#### QDCG Tax Rates (MFJ)

| Rate | Taxable Income (MFJ) |
|------|---------------------|
| 0% | $0 – $96,700 |
| 15% | $96,701 – $600,050 |
| 20% | Over $600,050 |

*(See 2025-tax-numbers.md for full bracket details)*

### Capital Gains and Losses (Line 7)

#### Schedule D and Form 8949
- Report sales of stocks, bonds, and other capital assets on **Form 8949**, then summarize on **Schedule D**. [Form 1040 Instructions (2025), Page 33]
- Form 8949 categories:
  - **Part I (Short-term):** Assets held 1 year or less — taxed at ordinary rates
  - **Part II (Long-term):** Assets held more than 1 year — taxed at QDCG rates
- Basis reporting: Form 1099-B Box 1e shows cost basis. If basis was reported to the IRS, use Box A (short-term) or Box D (long-term).

#### Capital Loss Limitation
- Net capital losses are deductible up to **$3,000/year** ($1,500 MFS). [IRC §1211(b)]
- Excess losses carry forward to future years indefinitely.
- Capital loss carryforward from prior years goes on Schedule D, Line 6 (short-term) or Line 14 (long-term).

#### Wash Sale Rule
- If you sell a security at a loss and buy a substantially identical security within 30 days before or after the sale, the loss is **disallowed**. [Pub 550 (2024)]
- The disallowed loss is added to the basis of the replacement security.
- Broker reports wash sale adjustments on Form 1099-B.

### U.S. Government Obligation Interest — Georgia Exemption
- Interest on U.S. Government obligations (T-bills, T-notes, T-bonds, savings bonds) is **exempt from Georgia income tax**. [IT-511 (2025), Schedule 1 Subtractions #4, p. 22]
- Report as a subtraction on GA Form 500 Schedule 1; the amount must be reduced by direct and indirect interest expenses attributable to the income.
- Dividends from mutual funds that invest in U.S. obligations are partially exempt — only the portion attributable to U.S. obligation interest can be subtracted.
- **FNMA (Fannie Mae), GNMA (Ginnie Mae), and FHLB interest is NOT exempt** from Georgia tax, nor is interest from repurchase agreements. [IT-511 (2025), Schedule 1 Subtractions #4, p. 22]
- Conversely, interest on **non-Georgia municipal bonds** (federally tax-exempt) must be ADDED to Georgia income. [IT-511 (2025), Schedule 1 Additions #1, p. 20]
