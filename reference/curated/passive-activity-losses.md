# Passive Activity Losses — Rental Real Estate (Form 8582)

## Source
- Instructions for Form 8582, Passive Activity Loss Limitations (2025), IRS Catalog Number 64294A, rev. Nov 26, 2025 — pages 1–13
- Publication 527 (2025), Residential Rental Property, Chapter 3 "Limits on Rental Losses" — pages 18–21

## Applicable To
- **Forms:** Form 8582 (Parts I–IX); Schedule E (Form 1040), Part I lines 21–22 and lines 23a–23e, line 43; Form 461 (excess business loss, after PAL); Form 6198 (at-risk, before PAL)
- **Workflow Steps:** Rental income/loss entry (Schedule E), loss limitation check, carryforward tracking, pre-filing audit

---

## Rules

### Default Rule — Rentals Are Passive
- Passive activities generally include: (1) trade or business activities in which you did not materially participate for the tax year, and (2) **"Rental activities, regardless of your participation."** [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 1]
- "PALs can't be used to offset income from nonpassive activities." A PAL occurs when total losses (including prior-year unallowed losses) from all passive activities exceed total income from all passive activities. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 1]
- "PALs not allowed in the current year are carried forward until they're allowed either against passive activity income; against the special allowance, if applicable; or when you sell or exchange your entire interest in the activity in a fully taxable transaction to an unrelated party." [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 1]
- Ordering: at-risk rules (Form 6198) apply **before** the passive loss rules; the excess business loss limitation (Form 461) applies **after**. Allowed passive capital losses may still be limited under §1211. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 2 "Coordination With Other Limitations"; Pub 527 (2025), Pages 18–19]
- Passive activity income does NOT include portfolio income (interest, dividends, annuities, royalties not derived in the ordinary course of a trade or business) or personal service income such as wages. Don't enter income that isn't passive activity income on Form 8582. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 7]

### Special $25,000 Allowance (Active Participation)
- "If you actively participated in a passive rental real estate activity, you may be able to deduct up to $25,000 of loss from the activity from your nonpassive income. This special allowance is an exception to the general rule disallowing losses in excess of income from passive activities." [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 4]
- **Active participation** (less stringent than material participation): "You may be treated as actively participating if, for example, you participated in making management decisions or arranged for others to provide services (such as repairs) in a significant and bona fide sense. Management decisions that may count as active participation include: Approving new tenants, Deciding on rental terms, Approving capital or repair expenditures, and Other similar decisions." [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 4]
- You are NOT considered to actively participate if at any time during the year your interest (including your spouse's) in the activity was **less than 10%** (by value) of all interests in the activity. Limited partners are not treated as actively participating. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 4; Pub 527 (2025), Page 20]
- Maximum special allowance [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 4]:

| Filing situation | Max allowance |
|---|---|
| Single, or MFJ | **$25,000** |
| MFS, lived apart from spouse ALL year | **$12,500** |
| MFS, lived with spouse at any time during the year | **$0** (not eligible; do not complete Part II) |

- "The special allowance isn't available if you were married, are filing a separate return for the year, and lived with your spouse at any time during the year." [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 4; Part II Caution, Page 10]
- **MAGI phase-out** [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 4 "Modified adjusted gross income limitation"]:
  - MAGI **$100,000 or less** ($50,000 or less MFS): loss deductible up to the full maximum allowance.
  - MAGI **more than $100,000 but less than $150,000** ($50,000/$75,000 MFS): "your special allowance is limited to 50% of the difference between $150,000 ($75,000 if married filing separately) and your modified adjusted gross income."
  - MAGI **$150,000 or more** ($75,000 or more MFS): "there is no special allowance."
- Pub 527 states the same allowance amounts and phase-out. [Pub 527 (2025), Page 20 "Maximum special allowance"]

### MAGI Definition for the Phase-Out (Form 8582, Line 6)
"To figure modified adjusted gross income, combine all the amounts used to figure adjusted gross income, except don't take into account:" [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 11, Line 6]
- Any passive activity loss as defined in section 469(d)(1)
- Any rental real estate loss allowed to real estate professionals
- The taxable amount of social security and tier 1 railroad retirement benefits
- Deductible contributions to traditional IRAs and section 501(c)(18) pension plans
- The deduction allowed for the deductible part of self-employment taxes
- The exclusion of interest from series EE and I U.S. savings bonds used to pay higher education expenses
- The exclusion of amounts received under an employer's adoption assistance program
- The student loan interest deduction
- The deduction allowed for foreign-derived intangible income (FDII) and global intangible low-taxed income (GILTI)

Also: include portfolio income/expenses clearly and directly allocable to portfolio income, income treated as nonpassive (e.g., overall gain from a PTP, recharacterized income), and "any overall loss from the entire disposition of a passive activity (considered a nonpassive loss)." [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 11, Line 6]

### EXCEPTIONS — Activities NOT Treated as Rental Activities (Short/Mid-Term Rentals)
"However, if you meet any of the five exceptions below, the rental of the property isn't treated as a rental activity." Exact text of the key exception: [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 3 "Rental Activities — Exceptions"]

> "An activity is not a rental activity if any of the following apply.
> 1. The average period of customer use is:
> a. 7 days or less, or
> b. 30 days or less and significant personal services were provided in making the rental property available for customer use."

- **Average period of customer use:** total number of days in all rental periods ÷ number of rentals during the tax year (multiple classes of property are weighted by gross rental income; see Regulations section 1.469-1(e)(3)(iii)). [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 3]
- **Significant personal services** "include only services performed by individuals. To determine if personal services are significant, all relevant facts and circumstances are taken into consideration, including the frequency of the services, the type and amount of labor required to perform the services, and the value of the services relative to the amount charged for use of the property." [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 3]
- The other three exceptions: (2) extraordinary personal services provided; (3) rental incidental to a nonrental activity (investment-holding: gross rental income < 2% of the smaller of unadjusted basis or FMV; or incidental to a trade/business); (4) property customarily made available during defined business hours for nonexclusive use by various customers; (5) property provided for use in a nonrental activity of a partnership/S corporation/joint venture in your capacity as an owner. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Pages 3–4]
- **Consequence if an exception applies** ("Reporting Income and Losses From the Activities"): "If an activity meets any of the five exceptions listed above, it's not a rental activity." You must then determine (1) whether the rental is a trade or business activity and (2) whether you materially participated. "If the activity is a trade or business activity in which you didn't materially participate, enter the income and losses from the activity in Part V" (i.e., still passive — but as a trade/business, **NOT** rental real estate, so NO $25,000 special allowance and no Part IV entry). "If the activity is a trade or business activity in which you did materially participate, report any income or loss from the activity on the forms or schedules normally used" — i.e., **nonpassive, fully deductible, not entered on Form 8582**. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 4]
- Practical reading: a typical Airbnb-style short-term rental (average stay ≤ 7 days) or a furnished mid-term rental (average stay ≤ 30 days) with significant services is NOT a "rental activity" under §469 — it gets no $25,000 allowance, and its losses are passive unless the owner materially participates; with material participation, losses are nonpassive. (Where to report — Schedule E vs. Schedule C — depends on whether substantial services are provided: "Generally, Schedule C is used when you provide substantial services in conjunction with the property." [Pub 527 (2025), Page 18])

### Material Participation — Tests for Individuals
"You materially participated for the tax year in an activity if you satisfy at least one of the following tests." [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 5]
1. You participated in the activity for **more than 500 hours**.
2. Your participation was **substantially all of the participation** in the activity of all individuals (including individuals who didn't own any interest in the activity) for the year.
3. You participated for **more than 100 hours** during the tax year, and you participated **at least as much as any other individual** (including individuals who didn't own any interest) for the year.
4. The activity is a **significant participation activity** (a trade/business with >100 hours of participation in which you don't otherwise materially participate), and your participation in all significant participation activities exceeds 500 hours.
5. You materially participated in the activity for **any 5 of the 10** immediately preceding tax years.
6. The activity is a **personal service activity** in which you materially participated for any 3 preceding tax years.
7. **Facts and circumstances** — regular, continuous, and substantial participation (not available if you participated 100 hours or less; managing doesn't count if anyone else was compensated for managing or spent more hours managing). [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 5]
- Spouse's participation counts as yours, even if the spouse owns no interest and even on a separate return. Work as an investor (reviewing financials, monitoring in a nonmanagerial capacity) doesn't count unless directly involved in day-to-day management. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 5]

### Real Estate Professional Exception
- Rental real estate activities in which you materially participated are NOT passive if you were a "real estate professional": (a) **more than half** of the personal services you performed in trades or businesses during the year were performed in real property trades or businesses in which you materially participated, AND (b) you performed **more than 750 hours** of services during the year in real property trades or businesses in which you materially participated. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 2; Pub 527 (2025), Page 19]
- If MFJ, **one spouse must separately meet both** requirements, without counting the other spouse's services. Employee services don't count unless you own more than 5% of the employer. Each rental real estate interest is a separate activity unless you elect to treat all interests as one activity. Real estate professionals complete Schedule E line 43. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 2; Pub 527 (2025), Page 19]

### Suspended Losses and Dispositions
- Prior-year unallowed losses are tracked **per activity** (Form 8582 Parts IV/V column (c), carried from column (c) of the prior-year Part VII / Part VIII column (b): "Keep a record of these amounts so the losses can be used to figure your PAL next year"). [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Pages 10, 13]
- **Complete disposition releases everything:** "If you disposed of your entire interest in a passive activity or a former passive activity to an unrelated person in a fully taxable transaction during the tax year, your losses allocable to the activity for the year aren't limited by the PAL rules." A fully taxable transaction is one in which you recognize all realized gain or loss. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 9 "Dispositions"]
- If you have an overall loss when combining the activity's income, losses, and prior-year unallowed losses in the year of complete disposition, "don't use Form 8582 for the activity. All losses (including prior-year unallowed losses) are allowed in full" on the forms and schedules normally used. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 9]
- Caution: "A disposition of less than substantially all of an entire interest doesn't trigger the allowance of prior-year unallowed losses." [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 9]

### When Form 8582 Is NOT Required
You don't have to file Form 8582 if you actively participated in rental real estate activities and you meet **all** of the following conditions [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 1 "Exception" under Who Must File; same list in Pub 527 (2025), Pages 20–21 "Form 8582 not required"]:
- Rental real estate activities with active participation were your only passive activities.
- You have no prior-year unallowed losses from these (or any other passive) activities.
- Your total loss from the rental real estate activities wasn't more than **$25,000** (**$12,500** if married filing separately).
- If MFS, you lived apart from your spouse all year.
- You have no current or prior-year unallowed credits from a passive activity.
- Your modified adjusted gross income was not more than **$100,000** (**$50,000** if married filing separately).
- You don't hold any interest in a rental real estate activity as a limited partner or as a beneficiary of an estate or a trust.

If all conditions are met, "your rental real estate losses are not limited, and you don't need to complete Form 8582. Enter losses reported on Schedule E (Form 1040), Supplemental Income and Loss, Part I, line 21, on Schedule E (Form 1040), Part I, line 22." [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 2]

### Form 8582 Structure — How Losses Flow
- **Parts IV and V feed Part I.** Part IV = rental real estate activities with active participation (columns: (a) current-year net income, (b) current-year net loss, (c) prior-year unallowed losses; totals go to Part I, lines 1a–1c). Part V = all other passive activities, including passive trade/business activities and "passive rental real estate activities that don't qualify for the special allowance" (totals go to Part I, lines 2a–2c). [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Pages 9–10]
- **Part I** combines net income and net loss from all passive activities (line 3, with line 1d/2d subtotals) to determine whether you have a PAL for 2025. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 9]
- **Part II** figures the special allowance: line 4 = smaller of the loss on Part I line 1d or line 3; line 5 = $150,000 ($75,000 MFS living apart); line 6 = MAGI; line 7 = line 5 minus line 6; line 8 = 50% of line 7, max $25,000 (don't enter more than $12,500 on line 8 if MFS living apart all year); line 9 = smaller of line 4 or line 8. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Pages 10–11]
- **Part III, line 11** = total losses allowed from all passive activities for 2025; use Parts IV–IX to figure the unallowed loss carried forward and the allowed loss to report on your forms and schedules. [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Page 11]
- **Parts VI–IX** allocate: Part VI allocates the Part II line 9 special allowance among rental real estate activities; Part VII allocates unallowed losses by ratio; Parts VIII/IX split each activity's allowed vs. unallowed losses (Part IX when an activity's losses appear on two or more forms/schedules). [Form 8582 Instructions (2025), Pages 12–13]
