# Mortgage Interest Deduction

## Source
- Publication 936 (2025), "Home Mortgage Interest Deduction," Catalog Number 10426G, Oct 28, 2025
- Instructions for Schedule A (Form 1040) (2025), Lines 8a–8d — IRS.gov/ScheduleA

## Applicable To
- **Forms:** Schedule A Lines 8a, 8d; Form 1040 Line 12a
- **Workflow Steps:** Step 6 (Deductions — Standard vs Itemized)

---

## Rules

### Basic Rule
- You can deduct home mortgage interest on your **main home** and one **second home** if you itemize deductions on Schedule A. [Pub 936 (2025), Page 2]
- The mortgage must be a **secured debt** on a **qualified home** in which you have an ownership interest.
- Both you and the lender must intend the loan to be repaid.

### Debt Limits

| Mortgage Origination Date | Debt Limit (MFJ) | Debt Limit (MFS) |
|---------------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| On or before Oct 13, 1987 ("grandfathered") | No limit | No limit |
| After Oct 13, 1987 and before Dec 16, 2017 | $1,000,000 | $500,000 |
| After Dec 15, 2017 | **$750,000** | **$375,000** |

[Pub 936 (2025), Page 2–3]

- The limit applies to the **combined** balance of mortgages on your main home and second home.
- For post-12/15/2017 debt, only **acquisition debt** qualifies — debt used to buy, build, or substantially improve a qualified home.

### Home Equity Loan Interest
- **NOT deductible** unless the loan proceeds were used to buy, build, or substantially improve the home that secures the loan. [Pub 936 (2025), Page 1, Reminders]
- This restriction is now **permanent** under OBBBA (previously set to expire).

### Mortgage Insurance Premiums (PMI)
- Under OBBBA (effective 2026+), mortgage insurance premiums associated with acquisition debt may be treated as deductible mortgage interest. [H&R Block OBBBA Summary]
- For 2025: The itemized deduction for mortgage insurance premiums has **expired** — you can no longer claim it for 2025. [Pub 936 (2025), Page 1, Reminders]

### Qualified Home
- A home includes a house, condo, co-op, mobile home, house trailer, or boat with sleeping, cooking, and toilet facilities. [Pub 936 (2025), Page 4]
- **Main home:** Where you ordinarily live most of the time.
- **Second home:** A home you choose to treat as your second home (need not be rented; if rented, must use personally for >14 days or >10% of rental days).

### Form 1098 Mapping
- Your mortgage lender sends **Form 1098** showing:
  - **Box 1:** Mortgage interest received → Schedule A, Line 8a
  - **Box 2:** Outstanding mortgage principal
  - **Box 4:** Refund of overpaid interest
  - **Box 5:** Mortgage insurance premiums (if applicable)
  - **Box 6:** Points paid on purchase

### Points
- Points paid on a loan to **buy** your main home are generally deductible in full in the year paid if several conditions are met (customary in the area, clearly designated as points, etc.). [Pub 936 (2025), Pages 6–8]
- Points paid on **refinancing** must generally be amortized over the life of the loan.
- Points shown in Box 6 of Form 1098 → Schedule A, Line 8a (if buying) or amortized portion on Line 8c (if refinancing).

### Special Situations
- **Late payment charges** on mortgage payments are deductible as mortgage interest. [Pub 936 (2025), Page 5]
- **Prepayment penalties** are deductible as mortgage interest. [Pub 936 (2025), Page 5]
- **Sale of home:** Deduct interest paid up to (but not including) the date of sale. [Pub 936 (2025), Page 5]
