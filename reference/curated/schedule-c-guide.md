# Schedule C — Business Income (Online Marketplace Business)

## Source
- Publication 17 (2025), "Your Federal Income Tax," Chapters 11–12 (Business Income, Business Expenses)
- Instructions for Schedule 1 (Form 1040) (2025), Line 3
- IRS Instructions for Schedule C (Form 1040) (2025)
- IRC §183 (Activities Not Engaged in for Profit — Hobby Loss)
- IRS Notice 2025-02 (Standard Mileage Rate)

## Applicable To
- **Forms:** Schedule C (Lines 1–31, Part III COGS); Schedule 1, Line 3; Form 1040, Line 8
- **Workflow Steps:** Step 3 (Business Income — Schedule C)

---

## Rules

### Business Profile Setup
- **Business code:** Select the NAICS code that best describes your activity. Look up your code in the [Census Bureau NAICS search](https://www.census.gov/naics/) or in the Schedule C instructions. Common codes for online sellers: **454110** (Electronic Shopping), **459510** (Used Merchandise Retailers). [Schedule C Instructions, Line B]
- **Accounting method:** Cash basis (report income when received, expenses when paid). [Schedule C Instructions, Line F]
- **Method of determining inventory:** Cost. [Schedule C Instructions, Part III]
- **Material participation:** Yes — if you personally perform the work or manage the business operations. [Schedule C Instructions, Line G]

### 1099-K Reconciliation
- **Line 1 (Gross receipts):** Enter the 1099-K gross amount. This is the total payments processed, **including refunds and sales tax collected by the platform**. [Schedule C Instructions, Line 1]
- **Line 2 (Returns and allowances):** Enter the total refunds/returns issued to buyers. This reconciles the 1099-K gross down to actual net receipts. [Schedule C Instructions, Line 2]
- **Line 3 (Net receipts):** Line 1 minus Line 2.
- **Sales tax:** Sales tax collected and remitted by the platform on your behalf is a pass-through — it does NOT appear on Schedule C as income or expense.

### Cost of Goods Sold (Part III)
- **Line 35:** Inventory at beginning of year (for year 1: $0).
- **Line 36:** Purchases less cost of items withdrawn for personal use.
- **Line 37:** Cost of labor (not applicable for sole proprietor's own labor).
- **Line 38:** Materials and supplies.
- **Line 40:** Other costs.
- **Line 41:** Add Lines 35–40.
- **Line 42:** Inventory at end of year.
- **Line 4 = Line 41 − Line 42:** COGS flows to Line 4 of Schedule C.
- COGS includes raw materials (purchase cost of items you resell) plus any materials used to prepare them for sale. [Pub 17 (2025), Chapter 12]

### Business Expenses (Part II)
Key deductible expenses:
- **Line 9 (Car and truck expenses):** Standard mileage rate **$0.70/mile** for 2025. Keep a mileage log with date, destination, purpose, and miles. [IRS Notice 2025-02]
  - **E-file requirement:** If Line 9 is non-zero, **Part IV (Lines 43–47) must be completed** or the return will be rejected (Business Rule SC-F1040-003). [IRS Schedule C Instructions, Part IV]
  - Form 4562 is **not required** when using the standard mileage rate with no depreciation on Line 13.
- **Line 10 (Commissions and fees):** Platform fees (transaction fees, processing fees, listing fees). [Schedule C Instructions, Line 10]
- **Line 22 (Supplies):** Shipping materials, packaging, tape, etc. [Schedule C Instructions, Line 22]
- **Line 27a (Other expenses):** Catch-all for other legitimate business expenses not listed elsewhere.

### Part IV — Information on Your Vehicle (Lines 43–47)
Required when **any amount is entered on Line 9**. Omitting Part IV triggers e-file rejection SC-F1040-003. [IRS Schedule C Instructions, Part IV]
- **Line 43:** Date vehicle was placed in service for business (MM/DD/YYYY).
- **Line 44a:** Total miles driven during the year (all purposes).
- **Line 44b:** Business miles included in Line 44a.
- **Line 44c:** Commuting miles included in Line 44a.
- **Line 45:** Was the vehicle available for personal use during off-duty hours? (Yes/No)
- **Line 46:** Do you (or your spouse) have another vehicle available for personal use? (Yes/No)
- **Line 47a:** Do you have evidence to support your deduction? (Yes/No)
- **Line 47b:** If yes, is the evidence written? (Yes/No)

**Documentation tip:** Reconstruct a mileage log from order records (dates, pickup locations) if you don't already have one. The IRS accepts reconstructed records when corroborated by other documentation (e.g., platform order history, receipts).

### Self-Employment Tax Threshold
- Schedule SE is required only if **net profit exceeds $400**. [IRC §1402]
- If Schedule C shows a **net loss**, no SE tax is due and Schedule SE is NOT required.

### Hobby Loss Rules (IRC §183)
- If the IRS determines an activity is a hobby (not engaged in for profit), losses cannot offset other income. [IRC §183]
- **Safe harbor:** An activity is presumed to be a business (not a hobby) if it shows a profit in **3 out of 5 consecutive years**. [IRC §183(d)]
- Factors the IRS considers: (1) manner carried on, (2) expertise, (3) time and effort, (4) expectation of asset appreciation, (5) prior success, (6) history of income/losses, (7) amount of occasional profits, (8) financial status, (9) elements of personal pleasure.
- **Documentation is key:** Keep business plan, marketing records, receipts, mileage log, and evidence of profit intent.

### Home Office / Business Use of Home (Form 8829)
- If you use part of your home (e.g., garage) **regularly and exclusively** for business, you may deduct a portion of home expenses. [Form 8829 Instructions]
- **Simplified method:** $5/sq ft, up to 300 sq ft = max $1,500 deduction.
- **Regular method:** Prorate mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, utilities by business-use percentage.
- Home office deduction **cannot create or increase a net loss** — it is limited to gross income from the business minus other expenses. Excess carries forward. [IRC §280A(c)(5)]
